How to write effective career summary?

Posted by Team SFS on September 02, 2024 · 1 min read

First two sentences of your resume are the most important. They should be able to grab the attention of the reader and make them want to read more. This is where your career summary comes into play. It is a brief statement that highlights your skills, experience, and accomplishments. It should be tailored to the job you are applying for and should be written in a way that makes you stand out from other candidates.

Common mistakes in writing career summary

  1. Being too vague: Your career summary should be specific and tailored to the job you are applying for. Avoid using generic statements that could apply to anyone.
  2. Use specific adjectives: Instead of saying you are a “hard worker” or a “team player”, use specific adjectives that describe your skills and experience.
  3. Try to use some numbers, percentages, or statistics to quantify your achievements.
  4. If you are applying for a job with more responsibilities, make sure your first two statements show that you are ready for the challenge.

You can use AI tools to craft a career summary, but do not rely completely on them. Make sure to review and edit the summary to make it more personalized and relevant to the job you are applying for.
